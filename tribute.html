Remembering Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President(Missile Man)

Early Life and Education

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931, to a Tamil Muslim family in the pilgrimage centre of Rameswaram on Pamban Island
After completing his education at Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram, Kalam went on to attend the St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli from where he graduated in Physics in 1954 and then Kalam moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology

The Early Career at ISRO and DRDO

Joining ISRO:

Dr. Kalam began his professional journey at the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) in 1963. He played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III, and the successful launch of the Rohini satellite marked a significant milestone in India's space program.

Missile Development Program:

In the 1980s, Kalam moved to the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO), where he led the Integrated Guided Missile Development Program. The successful development of missiles like Agni and Prithvi showcased India's technological prowess in defense capabilities.

Contributions to Indian Space Program

Dr. Kalam's brilliance in aerospace engineering played a crucial role in India's space achievements. He was a key figure to Successful Accomplishment of Chandrayaan-1 and Launch of Chandrayaan-2

"Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts, and thoughts result in action." - Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

The People's President

Presidential Tenure:

In 2002, Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. His presidency was marked by simplicity, accessibility, and a strong focus on education and youth development.

Educational Initiatives:

During his presidency, Kalam continued to emphasize the importance of education and launched several initiatives to promote scientific research and innovation, particularly among the youth. He believed that education was the key to national development.

Visionary Leader and Philosopher

Vision 2020:

Dr. Kalam was not only a scientist and statesman but also a visionary. He outlined his vision for India in the book "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium." The vision encompassed economic development, technological advancements, and social progress, with a strong emphasis on eradicating poverty.

A People's Philosopher:

Beyond his scientific and political contributions, Dr. Kalam was known for his philosophical outlook on life. His speeches and writings often reflected his deep spirituality and optimism, inspiring people to strive for personal excellence and contribute to the betterment of society.

Legacy and Endearing Memories

Legacy of Inspiration:

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam left an indelible mark on the hearts and minds of people worldwide. His life story is a testament to the power of dreams, hard work, and resilience. The youth, in particular, continue to draw inspiration from his words and deeds.

The Final Moments:

Tragically, Dr. Kalam breathed his last on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His passing was a profound loss, but his legacy continues to inspire and guide generations to come.
